## RAG is not the complete solution

Retrieval-Augmented Generation has become the standard architecture for incorporating external knowledge. It does improve factuality and reduce hallucination, but it's fundamentally a retrieval layer—it finds relevant information.

> RAG retrieves information. It does not understand it, validate it, normalize it, or determine what should happen next.

That gap between retrieval and action is where most real-world AI work happens.

## The complete pipeline

Production systems need layers beyond retrieval. Each layer adds value:

**Ingestion** → Bring data into the system, standardize formats, make it searchable

**Retrieval** → Find relevant documents and passages given a query

**Extraction** → Pull structured values from unstructured text (entities, dates, amounts, relationships)

**Validation** → Check extracted values against business rules, consistency constraints, and data quality standards

**Reasoning** → Apply domain logic to synthesize decisions from multiple data points

**Action** → Convert the result into the decision or output the system is designed to produce

Each stage depends on the previous one working correctly. Retrieval without validation wastes time on unverified information. Extraction without reasoning produces disconnected facts instead of insights.

## Domain intelligence: the missing layer

Domain intelligence is what sits between raw retrieval and business value. It includes:

- Entity extraction and normalization (recognizing that "Robert Smith" and "R. Smith" might be the same person)
- Consistency checking (flagging when retrieved data contradicts itself)
- Business rule application (enforcing policies specific to your domain)
- Confidence and uncertainty representation (being explicit about what you don't know)
- Provenance tracking (knowing where each piece of information came from)

This layer determines whether an AI system is reliable enough to use in production or just a research prototype.
