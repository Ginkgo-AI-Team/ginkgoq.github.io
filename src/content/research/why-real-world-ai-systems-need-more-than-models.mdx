## Benchmarks measure laboratory conditions

Model leaderboards show performance on curated, clean datasets under controlled conditions. Production systems face different challenges: data distribution shifts, incomplete context, edge cases that weren't in the benchmark, and users who need systems to acknowledge uncertainty rather than hallucinate.

A model that scores 95% on a benchmark might produce unreliable output in production when faced with:
- Out-of-distribution inputs
- Requests that fall outside the model's actual training distribution
- Scenarios where partial information should trigger a "I don't know" response instead of a guess

The benchmark gap isn't a failure of evaluation—it's a feature. Benchmarks can't capture everything that matters in production.

## Evaluation as system infrastructure

Real reliability comes from building evaluation into the system itself, not just running it once before deployment:

- **Regression testing**: Continuously verify that changes don't break known hard cases
- **Real-world measurement**: Track performance on actual production inputs, not just test sets
- **Confidence thresholds**: Route low-confidence outputs to human review automatically
- **Failure mode analysis**: Understand what types of inputs cause errors, and handle those explicitly

## System-level reliability beats model performance

The final output users rely on isn't determined by the model alone. It's determined by the entire system:

- **Retrieval quality**: Better source material produces better outputs
- **Input validation**: Catching malformed requests before they reach the model
- **Confidence representation**: Being explicit about uncertainty rather than overconfident guesses
- **Fallback behavior**: What happens when the primary approach fails
- **Observability**: Being able to trace failures back to their root cause
- **Provenance tracking**: Knowing where outputs came from and which data supports which conclusions

A 90% model inside a well-designed system often beats a 95% model inside a brittle architecture. Reliability emerges from the whole design, not just the component with the highest benchmark score.
